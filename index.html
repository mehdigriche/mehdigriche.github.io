Privacy Policy for Salaati Last updated: March 18, 2026 Overview Salaati ("the app") is a macOS menu bar application that displays Islamic prayer times. This policy explains what data the app accesses and how it is used. Data We Do Not Collect Salaati does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. There is no account system, no analytics, no tracking, and no advertising. Network Requests The app makes the following outbound requests to third-party services: api.aladhan.com — fetches daily prayer times based on the coordinates you provide. Your coordinates are sent as URL parameters. The Islamic Network operates this free public API. cdn.aladhan.com — streams athan audio files on demand. No identifying information is sent. geocoding-api.open-meteo.com — used when you search for a city by name. Only the city name you type is sent as a query parameter. ipapi.co — optionally used when you tap "Use My Location" to determine your approximate city and coordinates from your IP address. This is a one-time request triggered only by user action. No data is stored by the app after the location is set. All requests are made over HTTPS. Salaati does not log, store, or forward any data returned by these services. Location The app does not request GPS or CoreLocation permissions. Location is determined either by city search or by a voluntary IP-based lookup. The resulting coordinates are stored locally on your device in UserDefaults and are never transmitted to any server operated by the developer. Local Storage All app settings (prayer time preferences, athan selections, language, etc.) are stored locally on your device using macOS UserDefaults. This data never leaves your device. Third-Party Services The third-party APIs listed above have their own privacy policies: Islamic Network (AlAdhan): https://aladhan.com Open-Meteo: https://open-meteo.com/en/terms ipapi.co: https://ipapi.co/privacy Children Salaati does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children.
