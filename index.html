Privacy Policy for Salaati

Last updated: March 18, 2026

Overview

Salaati ("the app") is a macOS menu bar application that displays Islamic prayer times. This policy explains what data the app accesses and how it is used.

Data We Do Not Collect

Salaati does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. There is no account system, no analytics, no tracking, and no advertising.

Network Requests

The app makes the following outbound requests to third-party services:

  • api.aladhan.com — Fetches daily prayer times based on your coordinates. Coordinates are sent as URL parameters. Operated by Islamic Network.
  • cdn.aladhan.com — Streams athan audio files on demand. No identifying information is sent.
  • geocoding-api.open-meteo.com — Used when searching for a city. Only the city name you type is sent.
  • ipapi.co — Used only when you tap "Use My Location". Determines approximate city and coordinates via IP. One-time request only.

All requests are made over HTTPS. Salaati does not log, store, or forward any data returned by these services.

Location

The app does not request GPS or CoreLocation permissions. Location is determined either by city search or by a voluntary IP-based lookup.

The resulting coordinates are stored locally on your device using UserDefaults and are never transmitted to any server operated by the developer.

Local Storage

All app settings (prayer time preferences, athan selections, language, etc.) are stored locally on your device using macOS UserDefaults. This data never leaves your device.

Third-Party Services

  • Islamic Network (AlAdhan)
  • Open-Meteo
  • ipapi.co

Children

Salaati does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new date.
