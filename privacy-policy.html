Salaati — Privacy Policy

Prayer Times for macOS

Last updated: March 18, 2026

Overview

Salaati is a macOS menu bar application that displays Islamic prayer times. This page explains what data the app accesses and how it is used.

Short version: Salaati does not collect, store, or share any personal data. No account is required. No analytics. No ads. Ever.

What We Do Not Collect

  • ✓ Personal information
  • ✓ Account or login data
  • ✓ Analytics or usage data
  • ✓ Crash reports
  • ✓ Advertising identifiers
  • ✓ Location history
  • ✓ Device fingerprints
  • ✓ Any tracking data

Network Requests

The app makes outbound HTTPS requests to the following public APIs. No identifying information beyond what is described is sent.

  • api.aladhan.com
    Fetches daily prayer times using the coordinates you provide. Your coordinates are sent as URL query parameters. Operated by the Islamic Network.
  • cdn.aladhan.com
    Streams athan audio files on demand. No identifying information is sent.
  • geocoding-api.open-meteo.com
    Used when you search for a city by name. Only the city name you type is sent as a query string.
  • ipapi.co
    Optionally used when you tap "Use My Location" to determine your approximate city and coordinates from your IP address. This is a one-time request triggered only by explicit user action. No data from this response is stored by the developer.

Location

Salaati does not request GPS or CoreLocation permissions. Location is determined either by city name search or by a voluntary IP-based lookup.

The resulting coordinates are stored locally on your device in macOS UserDefaults and are never transmitted to any server operated by the developer.

Local Storage

All app settings — prayer preferences, athan reciter selections, language, adjustments, and background image path — are stored locally on your device using macOS UserDefaults. This data never leaves your device.

Third-Party Services
