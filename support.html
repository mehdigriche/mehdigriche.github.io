Getting Started

1
Launch Salaati. A crescent moon icon will appear in your macOS menu bar at the top right.
2
Click the icon to open the prayer times popover. You'll see today's prayer times and a live countdown.
3
Click Settings → Location to set your city. Search by name or tap "Use My Location".
4
Go to Settings → Athan to choose your reciter and enable notifications for each prayer.
5
Switch to Arabic mode anytime from Settings → General → Language.

Frequently Asked Questions

The app doesn't appear in my Dock — is that normal? ▾
Yes, this is intentional. Salaati lives in your menu bar (top-right corner of your screen), not in the Dock. Look for the crescent moon icon next to your other menu bar icons.
Prayer times seem wrong for my location ▾
First, check that your city is set correctly in Settings → Location. Then try changing the calculation method in Settings → Prayer → Fajr & Isha Method to match the authority followed in your country. You can also fine-tune individual prayer times in Settings → Adjust.
The athan is not playing ▾
Check that: (1) Silent mode is off in Settings → Athan, (2) the bell icon next to that prayer is red (not grey) in the main popover, (3) your Mac volume is not muted, and (4) you have an active internet connection — athan audio streams from cdn.aladhan.com.
I'm receiving too many notifications ▾
Each prayer fires one notification per day. If you're seeing duplicates, try going to Settings → Athan → Configure in System Settings and check your notification settings. You can also disable notifications per prayer using the bell icon in the main popover.
How do I stop the athan while it's playing? ▾
Click the Salaati icon in the menu bar to open the popover. A red Stop Athan bar will appear at the bottom of the popover — tap it to stop playback immediately.
How do I change the Hijri date? ▾
Go to Settings → Adjust → Hijri Date Adjustment. Use the stepper to offset the date by ±30 days to match your local moon sighting authority.
Can I add a background image? ▾
Yes — go to Settings → General → Background Image, toggle it on, and click "Choose Image" to pick any photo from your Mac. You can adjust the opacity so it stays subtle.
How do I make Salaati launch at startup? ▾
Go to Settings → General → Start at Login and toggle it on. Salaati will launch automatically every time you restart your Mac.
Does Salaati work offline? ▾
Prayer times are fetched from the internet once per day and cached locally. The app will continue to show today's prayer times without internet. However, athan audio streams live and requires a connection — no internet means no sound.
Does the app collect any of my data? ▾
No. Salaati does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
